Derelict

Derelict is a game developed by me and five of my peers as our capstone project during college. The project spanned three months—three weeks of documentation followed by ten weeks of development. Derelict is a roguelike first-person shooter set aboard a rusted, derelict spaceship overrun by hostile robots. The core mechanic revolves around programmable weapons that can be modified to create destructive and highly personalized loadouts. We drew inspiration from Doom, Noita, and Quake, believing a fusion of their mechanics and style would result in a unique experience we hadn’t seen done before.

About this game

Game Features:

  • Five distinct weapons and 37 unique modifiers, allowing players to create wild and strategic weapon combinations.
  • A dynamic weapon loadout system where modifiers run in sequence—from bottom to top—giving players precise control over how their weapons behave.
  • MModifiers range from altering projectile stats and types to passive effects like healing, and even meta modifiers that duplicate the effect of previous ones.
  • Procedurally generated map for a unique map layout for each playthrough.
  • Steam Audio integration providing a realistic 3D audio experience to enhance immersion.
  • Intelligent AI enemies that react to combat situations—taking cover when low on ammo or holding angles for tactical advantage.

My Contributions

I worked on various aspects of the project, including programming, modeling, and texturing.

  • Early in development, I implemented the base movement, weapon pickup, shooting, reloading, and inventory system for the player. These systems were later iterated on and replaced.
  • I designed and implemented the main menu scene, including its layout, transitions, and navigation to settings and credits.
  • I modeled all five weapons, created all 37 modifier icons, and designed multiple environmental textures, such as wall textures and floor panels.
  • I also modeled and textured the in-game chest containers.
  • On the UI side, I created the HUD health bar and the weapon ammo display.

My Thoughts

Overall Im very happy with the way this game turned out, especially how good and fun it is given the 10 week production deadline. I wish we could have continued working on this project as there were many features we planned on adding but due to time contraints they had to be cut from the final product, some features we didnt start making yet. I think this game has lots of potential and could make for an even more interesting game given more time to work on it.
